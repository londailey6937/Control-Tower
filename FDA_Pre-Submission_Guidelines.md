# FDA Q-Submission & Pre-Submission Guidelines

**Extracted from:** FDA Webinar — Q-Submission Program for Medical Device Submissions
**Date:** June 11, 2019
**Presenters:** Susannah Gilbert (Acting Policy Analyst, CDRH) & Josh Nipper (Director, Division of Submission Support)
**Guidance Document:** "Requests for Feedback and Meetings for Medical Device Submissions: The Q-Submission Program" (Final, May 7, 2019)

---

## 1. What Is the Q-Submission Program?

- A **voluntary program** providing a mechanism to request different types of interactions with FDA regarding medical device submissions.
- The request submitted to FDA is called a **Q-Submission (Q-Sub)**.
- Interactions can consist of **written feedback**, a **face-to-face meeting**, or a **teleconference**, depending on topic and submitter preference.
- All Q-Submissions, regardless of type, must be **formally submitted in electronic copy (eCopy) format** to FDA via the Document Control Center.

---

## 2. Types of Q-Submissions

### Five Main Q-Sub Types (Covered in Detail)

| Q-Sub Type                                  | Purpose                                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Pre-Submission Meeting Request**          | Written feedback + subsequent meeting (teleconference or face-to-face)                               |
| **Pre-Submission Written Feedback Request** | Written feedback only (no meeting)                                                                   |
| **Submission Issue Request (SIR)**          | Discuss outstanding review issues from a hold letter, IDE letter, or IND clinical hold letter        |
| **Informational Meeting Request**           | Present information to FDA without expectation of official feedback                                  |
| **Study Risk Determination**                | Obtain significant risk (SR) or non-significant risk (NSR) determination for a future clinical study |

### Additional Q-Sub Types (Covered Elsewhere)

- PMA Day 100 Meetings
- Breakthrough Device Designation Requests
- Interactions for Breakthrough Devices
- Early Collaboration Meetings
- Accessory Classification Requests

## 3. Pre-Submission (Pre-Sub) Guidelines

### 3.1 Purpose

- A formal request to FDA for feedback **prior to** an intended premarket submission, IDE, accessory classification request, or CLIA Waiver.
- Used to help **guide product development**, **develop study protocols**, or **prepare an application**.

### 3.2 Applicable Submission Types

Pre-Subs can be submitted to discuss potential or planned:

- 510(k)s
- PMAs
- De Novo Requests
- HDEs
- IDEs
- BLAs
- INDs
- Accessory Classification Requests
- CLIA Waivers

### 3.3 Content Requirements

- **Include specific questions** so FDA can provide useful feedback.
- **Limit to 3–4 substantial topics** for the most productive conversations.
- Only include information **relevant to the specific questions** being asked — you do not need to complete all testing or data collection beforehand.
- Include enough information for the review team to understand what you are asking.
- Specify whether you are requesting **written feedback only** or a **meeting** (which also includes written feedback prior to the meeting).

### 3.4 Pre-Submission Checklist

- An updated Pre-Submission checklist is provided in **Appendix 1** of the guidance document.
- Examples of questions that can lead to productive interactions are in **Appendix 2**.

### 3.5 MDUFA Review Timelines

| Milestone                         | Timeline                                                            |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Acceptance review (RTA) completed | Within **15 days** of receiving the Pre-Sub                         |
| Meeting date determined           | By **Day 30**                                                       |
| Written feedback provided         | **5 days before the meeting** or by **Day 70**, whichever is sooner |

### 3.6 FDA Feedback Commitment

- FDA intends that feedback provided in response to a Pre-Sub **will not change**, provided:
  - The information in a future IDE or marketing submission is **consistent** with that provided in the Pre-Sub, and
  - The data or other information in the future submission **do not raise any important new issues** materially affecting safety or effectiveness.

### 3.7 Cost

- **There are no costs** associated with Q-Submissions or Pre-Submissions at this time.

## 4. Practical Guidance from the Q&A Session

### 4.1 Multiple Pre-Submissions for the Same Device

- You **can** submit multiple Pre-Subs for the same device on different topics over time.
- Submissions for the same device with the same intended use are tracked as **supplements** within the same Q-Sub family, so the review team keeps information together.
- **Avoid** having multiple Pre-Subs for the same product and same indication open simultaneously — this may split review resources and result in inconsistent feedback.
- **Exception:** If the same device has two completely different indications (e.g., cardiac and neuro), two separate Pre-Subs to two different review groups is acceptable.

### 4.2 Written Feedback vs. Meeting — Best Practice

- **Request a meeting upfront**, even if you think written feedback may suffice.
- It is **easier to cancel** a scheduled meeting than to add one after the fact.
- If written feedback is sufficient, you can cancel the meeting — this is perfectly acceptable to FDA.
- If you initially request only written feedback but later want a meeting, scheduling becomes difficult and may take longer.

### 4.3 Meeting Format

- Meetings are typically scheduled for **60 minutes**.
- Longer meetings are possible but not standard; 60 minutes is considered most productive.
- **In-person vs. teleconference** is a personal/sponsor decision:
  - In-person allows demos, face-to-face interaction
  - Teleconference may be scheduled sooner

### 4.4 Meeting Minutes

- Meeting minutes are **required** for all meetings, regardless of Q-Sub type.
- The **submitter is responsible** for drafting meeting minutes.
- FDA has approximately **30 days** to review and respond to submitted meeting minutes.

### 4.5 Disagreements with FDA Feedback

- Pre-Sub feedback is **not binding** — submitters may choose a different path.
- There is **no formal appeal process** or least-burdensome flag for Pre-Sub feedback.
- If you disagree with feedback:
  1. **First:** Reach out to the lead reviewer
  2. **Then:** Escalate to the assistant director (formerly branch chief)
  3. **Then:** Escalate to the division director
  4. You may also raise concerns in the **cover letter** of your marketing submission
- The least-burdensome flag is only available **after** a deficiency/AI letter for a 510(k) — not at the Pre-Sub stage.

### 4.6 When a Pre-Sub Is (and Isn't) Needed

- Pre-Subs are appropriate for substantive questions about regulatory strategy, clinical protocols, predicate selection, testing plans, etc.
- **Not every question requires a Pre-Sub.** Simple questions (e.g., "What supplement type should I use?") can be addressed by reaching out to the lead reviewer or branch management directly.
- If FDA determines your question needs deeper analysis, they may direct you to submit a formal Pre-Sub.

### 4.7 Regulatory Classification Questions

- If your primary question is "Am I a medical device?" or "What classification am I?" — the **513(g) program** is the appropriate pathway for a formal determination.
- However, you can use a Pre-Sub or Informational Meeting to discuss your product, get general suggestions, and understand how intended use claims affect classification.
- You would **not** get a formal classification decision under the Pre-Sub program.

### 4.8 Confidentiality

- All information submitted through the Q-Sub program is considered **confidential**.

## 5. Submission Issue Requests (SIRs)

### 5.1 Purpose

- Discuss outstanding review issues provided in a marketing submission hold letter, IDE letter, or IND clinical hold letter.
- Help submitters determine how to respond to outstanding questions in formal responses.

### 5.2 When to Use (and Not Use)

- **Use** when you need to discuss approaches to address deficiencies identified in a formal letter.
- **Do not use** for simple clarification questions that can be handled by the lead reviewer.
- **Do not use** to discuss issues while a file is under active review (use interactive review instead).

### 5.3 Two-Tiered Review Timeline

| Timing                                                         | FDA Feedback Goal |
| -------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------- |
| SIR received **within 60 days** of the associated letter       | **21 days**       |
| SIR received **more than 60 days** after the associated letter | **70 days**       |

**Rationale:** When received promptly, issues are still current and the review team is familiar with them. After extended time, the team may need to re-familiarize themselves, adding processing time.

### 5.4 Key Changes

- Formerly called "Submission Issue Meetings (SIMs)" — renamed because feedback can now be written or via meeting.
- SIRs and Informational Meetings **no longer receive an RTA (acceptance) review**.

## 6. Key Takeaways for Your Gyroscope Balance Belt

Given that your device has **no clear predicate** and would likely pursue a **De Novo** pathway:

1. **Submit a Pre-Sub** before your De Novo request to get FDA feedback on:
   - Device classification and regulatory pathway confirmation
   - Appropriate performance testing (bench, electrical safety, biocompatibility)
   - Clinical study design (endpoints, patient population, study size)
   - Software documentation requirements (if electronically controlled)

2. **Limit your Pre-Sub to 3–4 topics** for the most productive interaction.

3. **Request a meeting** (not just written feedback) — you can always cancel if written feedback is sufficient.

4. **No cost** is associated with the Pre-Sub.

5. **Target the Physical Medicine Devices division** (21 CFR Part 890) when submitting.
